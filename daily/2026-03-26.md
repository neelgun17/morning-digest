## Learning Block (~30 min)

### 1. LLM Memory & Context (12 min read)
**"How LLMs Handle Infinite Context With Finite Memory"** — Towards Data Science

A deep dive into how models like Google's Infini-attention solve the memory paradox: instead of storing entire conversations, they compress history into fixed-size matrices the model can reference. Covers the gap between advertised context windows and actual effective context, and why a 1M-token window doesn't mean 1M tokens of useful reasoning.

- [Read the article](https://towardsdatascience.com/llms-can-now-process-infinite-context-windows/)

*Why this was picked: Directly addresses your core interest — how LLMs decide what memory is valuable. This is foundational for understanding the space.*

### 2. Philosophy — Introduction to the Big 3 (10 min read)
**"An Introduction to the Big 3: Stoicism, Existentialism, Absurdism"** — Medium

A clear, beginner-friendly overview of three major philosophical frameworks and how they differ in their approach to meaning, suffering, and the good life. Great starting map before you go deeper into any one branch.

- [Read the article](https://medium.com/the-avenue-of-absurdism/an-introduction-to-the-big-3-stoicism-existentialism-absurdism-c8aa2d421e4d)

*Why this was picked: Gives you a lay of the land across philosophy's most accessible schools. Pairs well with the themes in The Count of Monte Cristo (justice, purpose, suffering).*

### 3. Sculpture — How Sculptures Are Made (8 min read)
**"Sculpture: Modeling, Materials, Techniques"** — Britannica

Covers the core processes: carving (subtractive), modeling (additive), casting (bronze/resin), and assembly. Explains how sculptors think about form from the inside out. Authoritative reference you can revisit.

- [Read the article](https://www.britannica.com/art/sculpture/Modeling)

*Why this was picked: Foundational overview of the actual craft. Understanding the "how" makes appreciating specific sculptors much richer.*
